Pegasus Bridge

The small bridges that were the crux of Operation Overlord

Glider's #1 #2 and #3 after landing near the bridge on June 6th, 1944

As the first allied soldiers to step foot in mainland Europe, the 181-man strong force of the 6th airborne attacked the Caen Canal bridge and the Orne River bridge using a coup de main operation. 6 gliders flew in at 0016 hours and by 0026 hours the bridges were under British control. The following 6 hours were full of intense fighting to defend the bridge, and the western flank of Operation Overlord.

John Howard

John Howard was the company commander and led Glider #1. He orchestrated the operation while leading his men to capture and defend the Canal Bridge.

Den Brotheridge

Den Brotheridge led Glider #2. He is known as the first allied fatality due to german fire in Operation Overlord. He was shot and killed running across the bridge mere minutes from the beginning of the raid.

David Wood

David Wood led Glider #3 which was in charge of defeating the bridge security and defense forces.

Dennis Fox

Dennis Fox led Glider #4. Along with Glider #6, they attacked and controlled the River Bridge.

Tony Hooper

Tony Hooper led Glider #5. This glider mistakingly landed on the wrong LZ 20 km away from Benouville and Pegasus Bridge.

Wally Parr

Wally Par led Glider #6. He lead his group to attack the river bridge.

Timeline of Operation Deadstick

  • 0016 - Glider's #1, #2, #3, #4, #6 land on point. #5 glider lands 20 km east on Dives River by mistake.
  • 0026 - Bridges are captured, losing only 2 men in process.
  • 0100 - Germans begin counter-attack measures
  • 0300 - 7th Parachute Battalion reinforces the Glider units.
  • 0900 - Snipers begin pinning the British down. 2 gunboats attack the bridge via the river.
  • 1330 - The sound of bagpipes signals the arrival of Lord Lovat's Commando units to take over the defence of the bridges.
